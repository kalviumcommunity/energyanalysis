Daily Consumption Trend

Insight: Daily consumption fluctuates between roughly 74 kWh and 90 kWh. Weekend days consistently show elevated totals due to increased residential activity, while mid-week values dip as commercial loads dominate. The overall 30-day trend remains stable, with no clear upward or downward drift.

Hourly Average Consumption

Insight: Two distinct peaks emerge — a morning peak between 06:00 and 09:00 (driven by HVAC start-up and lighting) and a stronger evening peak from 17:00 to 21:00 (cooking, entertainment, EV charging). Overnight hours (00:00–05:00) represent base load at roughly 40 % of peak demand. Red bars highlight these peak windows.

Day-of-Week Distribution

Insight: Saturday and Sunday medians sit approximately 9 % above weekday medians, confirming the expected weekend uplift. Interquartile ranges are wider on weekends, reflecting more variable usage patterns. Outlier dots above the whiskers correspond to the injected spike anomalies.

Spike Detection (IQR Method)

Insight: The IQR method flagged 20 data points as outliers (red dots above the amber threshold line at 3.72 kWh). These spikes are distributed across the entire 30-day window without clustering, suggesting random equipment faults or meter glitches rather than systematic load issues. Utility teams should investigate any cluster of 3+ consecutive spikes as potential equipment failure.

Assumptions & Limitations

  • Synthetic data: The dataset was programmatically generated with controlled noise; real-world data would exhibit seasonal trends, weather correlations, and tariff-driven behaviour shifts.
  • Single IQR threshold: A global IQR bound was used. In production, per-meter or time-segmented thresholds would reduce false positives during known high-load events.
  • Linear interpolation: Missing values were filled linearly. For larger gaps, spline or seasonal decomposition methods (STL) may be more appropriate.
  • No external variables: Factors like holidays, tariff changes, or occupancy schedules were not modelled.
  • Fixed 30-min granularity: Smart meters can report at 1-min or 15-min intervals; aggregation level affects peak detection sensitivity.
  • Weekend definition: Saturday and Sunday are treated as weekends; this may differ by region or industry.
